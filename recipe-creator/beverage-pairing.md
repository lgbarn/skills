# Beverage Pairing & Custom Drink Creation

Reference for pairing drinks with food and creating original beverages from
scratch. Load this file when a user asks what to drink with a meal, wants a
beverage recommendation, or wants to create a custom drink — alcoholic or
non-alcoholic.

---

## Pairing Principles

The same science behind food pairing applies to beverages: complement, contrast,
or bridge flavors between the plate and the glass.

### The Three Pairing Strategies

| Strategy | How It Works | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Complement** | Drink echoes a flavor in the food | Oaky Chardonnay with butter-sauced fish (both have lactone/butter notes) |
| **Contrast** | Drink opposes a dominant flavor to create balance | Crisp, acidic Sauvignon Blanc cuts through rich, creamy pasta |
| **Bridge** | Drink shares a compound with a third element in the dish | Pinot Noir (earthy) bridges mushroom risotto and Parmesan (both earthy-umami) |

### Universal Pairing Rules

These work across wine, beer, cocktails, and non-alcoholic drinks:

1. **Acid matches acid** — an acidic drink pairs with bright, acidic food.
   A flat, mellow drink next to a citrus-dressed salad feels lifeless.
2. **Fat loves tannin and bubbles** — tannins (red wine, black tea) and
   carbonation both cut through richness. Steak + Cabernet. Fried chicken +
   sparkling water or Champagne. BBQ + hoppy IPA.
3. **Sweet must match or exceed the food's sweetness** — a dry wine next to
   a sweet dessert makes the wine taste sour and thin. The drink should be
   at least as sweet as the food.
4. **Spice + alcohol = more heat** — alcohol amplifies capsaicin. With spicy
   food, choose low-alcohol or off-dry (slightly sweet) drinks. Or go
   non-alcoholic: lassi, coconut water, lemonade.
5. **Weight matches weight** — a light dish needs a light drink; a heavy dish
   can handle a bold one. Delicate fish + heavy Cabernet is a mismatch.
   Braised short ribs + a light Pinot Grigio disappears.
6. **Smoke loves sweet and malt** — smoked meats pair beautifully with
   malty beers, bourbon, or sweet tea. The sweetness and smoke echo each other.
7. **Regional pairing is a cheat code** — what grows together goes together.
   Italian food + Italian wine. Thai food + Thai iced tea. Mexican food +
   mezcal or agua fresca. Centuries of co-evolution already did the work.

## Wine Pairing

### Quick Reference by Food Type

| Food | Wine Style | Why | Specific Picks |
|------|-----------|-----|----------------|
| **Grilled red meat** | Bold red with tannins | Tannins bind to meat proteins, softening the wine and cleansing the palate | Cabernet Sauvignon, Malbec, Syrah/Shiraz |
| **Braised/stewed meat** | Medium-full red, earthy | Earthy, soft wines echo the slow-cooked richness | Pinot Noir, Burgundy, Barolo, Tempranillo |
| **Fried chicken / fried foods** | Sparkling or crisp white | Bubbles and acid cut through oil | Champagne, Prosecco, dry Riesling, Cava |
| **Grilled/roasted chicken** | Medium white or light red | Versatile protein, don't overpower | Chardonnay (unoaked), Viognier, Beaujolais |
| **Fish (light, white)** | Light, crisp white | Match delicacy; acid brightens | Sauvignon Blanc, Pinot Grigio, Muscadet, Albariño |
| **Fish (rich, oily — salmon)** | Fuller white or light red | Match the richness of the fish | Oaked Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, rosé |
| **Shellfish** | Crisp white, mineral | Clean, mineral wines echo the sea | Chablis, Muscadet, Vermentino |
| **Pasta (tomato sauce)** | Medium red, good acidity | Acid in wine matches acid in tomato | Sangiovese (Chianti), Barbera, Montepulciano |
| **Pasta (cream sauce)** | Fuller white | Fat + acid + body | Oaked Chardonnay, Viognier |
| **Spicy food (Thai, Indian, Mexican)** | Off-dry white or rosé | Residual sugar tempers heat; low alcohol | Gewurztraminer, off-dry Riesling, rosé |
| **Soul food (greens, mac & cheese)** | Off-dry Riesling or sparkling | Acid and slight sweetness balance the rich, smoky flavors | Riesling Kabinett, Crémant, Lambrusco |
| **BBQ / smoked meats** | Bold red or Zinfandel | Smoky, jammy wine echoes the smoke | Zinfandel, Shiraz, Malbec, Petite Sirah |
| **Cheese (hard, aged)** | Bold red or fortified | Umami in cheese + tannin = savory match | Cabernet, aged Rioja, Port |
| **Cheese (soft, creamy)** | Sparkling or light white | Bubbles cut through cream | Champagne, Prosecco, Chenin Blanc |
| **Chocolate desserts** | Sweet red or fortified | Must be at least as sweet; shared roasted notes | Port, Banyuls, Pedro Ximénez, late harvest Zin |
| **Fruit desserts** | Sweet white or sparkling | Fruit + fruit, with enough sweetness | Moscato d'Asti, Sauternes, late harvest Riesling |

### Wine Body Spectrum (Lightest → Fullest)

**Whites**: Pinot Grigio → Sauvignon Blanc → Riesling → Chenin Blanc →
Chardonnay (unoaked) → Viognier → Chardonnay (oaked)

**Reds**: Gamay (Beaujolais) → Pinot Noir → Grenache → Sangiovese → Merlot →
Tempranillo → Syrah → Cabernet Sauvignon → Malbec → Petite Sirah

## Beer Pairing

### Pairing by Beer Style

| Beer Style | Character | Pairs With |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Pilsner / Lager** | Light, crisp, clean, refreshing | Fried foods, seafood, light salads, hot dogs, tacos |
| **Wheat beer (Hefeweizen)** | Fruity, spicy, effervescent | Salads, light seafood, brunch, sausage, soft cheese |
| **Pale Ale / IPA** | Hoppy, bitter, citrusy | Burgers, BBQ, spicy food (bitterness resets palate), strong cheese |
| **Amber / Red Ale** | Malty, caramel, balanced | Roasted chicken, grilled pork, pizza, Thanksgiving dinner |
| **Brown Ale** | Nutty, malty, gentle | Stews, braised meats, mushroom dishes, soul food |
| **Porter** | Roasty, chocolate, medium body | Smoked meats, chocolate desserts, grilled vegetables, chili |
| **Stout** | Rich, coffee, dark chocolate | Oysters (classic), braised short ribs, rich desserts, oxtails |
| **Belgian Dubbel/Tripel** | Fruity, spicy, complex, high alcohol | Rich stews, aged cheese, duck, Moroccan tagine |
| **Sour / Gose** | Tart, bright, refreshing | Ceviche, goat cheese, fruit desserts, salads, Thai food |
| **Saison** | Dry, peppery, farmhouse | Grilled chicken, roasted vegetables, mussels, charcuterie |

### The Beer + Fried Chicken Rule

Fried chicken is the single most versatile beer-pairing food. Almost any beer
works because the fat, salt, and crunch reset the palate between sips. But
the top three:
1. **Pilsner**: clean, crisp, the classic (think Church's + an ice-cold beer)
2. **IPA**: hops cut through the oil; citrus notes echo hot sauce
3. **Stout**: roasted notes complement the crust's Maillard flavors

## Cocktail Pairing & Creation

### Cocktail Pairing by Food

| Food Style | Cocktail Direction | Examples |
|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| **Rich, fatty** | Bright, spirit-forward, citrus | Old Fashioned, Negroni, Whiskey Sour |
| **Spicy** | Sweet-tart, lower alcohol, tropical | Margarita, Paloma, rum punch, piña colada |
| **Light, fresh (seafood, salad)** | Herbal, clean, gin-based | Gin & Tonic, Tom Collins, French 75, Gimlet |
| **Smoky (BBQ, grilled)** | Whiskey or mezcal-based, dark | Mezcal Negroni, Bourbon Smash, Smoky Margarita |
| **Sweet/dessert** | After-dinner, spirit-forward | Espresso Martini, Brandy Alexander, Amaretto Sour |
| **Soul food** | Bourbon, sweet tea-based, lemonade | Bourbon Lemonade, Sweet Tea Bourbon, Whiskey Peach Smash |
| **Brunch** | Sparkling, fruity, low proof | Mimosa, Bellini, Aperol Spritz, Bloody Mary |

### Building a Custom Cocktail from Scratch

The cocktail creation framework mirrors recipe creation — there's a structure,
and once you know it, you can improvise endlessly.

#### The 5-Component Framework

Every great cocktail is built from up to 5 components. Not all are required,
but the balance between them is:

| Component | Role | Examples | Typical Ratio |
|-----------|------|---------|---------------|
| **Base spirit** | The backbone — dominant flavor | Bourbon, gin, rum, tequila, vodka, mezcal | 2 oz |
| **Sweet** | Balances acid and bitterness, adds body | Simple syrup, honey syrup, liqueur, agave, fruit | 0.5–1 oz |
| **Sour / Acid** | Brightens, creates liveliness, balances sweet | Lemon juice, lime juice, grapefruit, shrub, vinegar | 0.75–1 oz |
| **Bitter / Modifier** | Adds complexity and depth | Bitters (Angostura, Peychaud's), amaro, Campari, vermouth | Dashes to 1 oz |
| **Dilution / Lengthener** | Adjusts strength, adds texture | Ice (shaking/stirring), soda water, tonic, ginger beer | To taste / to fill |

#### The Classic Ratios

| Template | Ratio | Examples |
|----------|-------|---------|
| **Sour** | 2 spirit : 1 sour : 0.75 sweet | Whiskey Sour, Daiquiri, Margarita, Sidecar |
| **Spirit-forward** | 2 spirit : 1 sweet modifier : dashes bitter | Old Fashioned, Manhattan, Negroni |
| **Highball** | 2 spirit : fill with lengthener | Gin & Tonic, Rum & Coke, Whiskey Ginger, Paloma |
| **Fizz** | Sour template + soda | Tom Collins, French 75, Aperol Spritz |
| **Smash** | 2 spirit : 0.75 sweet : muddled fresh fruit/herbs | Whiskey Smash, Mojito, Bourbon Peach Smash |

#### Spirit Selection Guide

| Spirit | Flavor Profile | Pairs Best With | Avoid With |
|--------|---------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Bourbon** | Caramel, vanilla, oak, warm | Stone fruit, honey, brown sugar, bitters, fall/winter flavors | Delicate citrus (overpowers) |
| **Rye whiskey** | Spicy, dry, peppery | Ginger, cherry, apple, bitters, vermouth | Heavy cream (clashes with the spice) |
| **Gin** | Juniper, botanical, herbal, citrusy | Cucumber, elderflower, citrus, tonic, herbs | Strong tropical fruit (muddies the botanicals) |
| **Vodka** | Neutral — a canvas | Anything — it carries other flavors | Nothing (that's its superpower) |
| **White rum** | Light, sweet, slightly grassy | Tropical fruit, lime, mint, coconut, ginger | Heavy bitters (too delicate) |
| **Dark/aged rum** | Caramel, molasses, spice, tropical | Pineapple, coconut, brown sugar, lime, allspice | Nothing pairs poorly (very versatile) |
| **Tequila (blanco)** | Agave, bright, peppery, citrusy | Lime, grapefruit, chili, salt, agave, cilantro | Heavy dairy |
| **Mezcal** | Smoky, earthy, complex | Pineapple, grapefruit, chocolate, chili, mango | Overly sweet mixers (smoke needs edge) |
| **Brandy/Cognac** | Fruity, warm, oaky, refined | Apple, pear, cinnamon, honey, lemon | Fizzy mixers (wastes complexity) |
| **Scotch** | Smoky (peated) or honeyed (unpeated) | Honey, lemon, ginger, drambuie | Overly sweet — let the spirit speak |

#### Syrup Crafting

Custom syrups are the fastest way to make a cocktail unique. The base method
is always the same: equal parts sugar + liquid, heated until dissolved.

**Base method (simple syrup):**
1:1 granulated sugar : water. Heat gently, stir until dissolved. Cool.
Keeps 2–4 weeks refrigerated.

**Rich simple syrup:** 2:1 sugar : water. Thicker, sweeter, longer shelf life.
Better for spirit-forward drinks where you want sweetness without dilution.

| Syrup | How to Make | Pairs With |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **Honey syrup** | 1:1 honey : warm water (honey is too thick alone) | Bourbon, rye, gin, lemon, ginger |
| **Demerara syrup** | 1:1 demerara sugar : water | Dark rum, bourbon, Old Fashioneds |
| **Ginger syrup** | Simmer sliced ginger in simple syrup 20 min, strain | Bourbon, rum, tequila, vodka, whiskey |
| **Cinnamon syrup** | 2 cinnamon sticks in simple syrup, simmer 10 min, strain | Bourbon, dark rum, apple, pear |
| **Lavender syrup** | 2 tbsp dried lavender in simple syrup, steep 15 min, strain | Gin, vodka, lemon, champagne |
| **Rosemary syrup** | 3 sprigs rosemary in simple syrup, simmer 10 min, strain | Gin, grapefruit, lemon, sparkling water |
| **Jalapeño syrup** | Slice 2 jalapeños into simple syrup, simmer 5 min, strain | Tequila, mezcal, margarita, pineapple |
| **Hibiscus syrup** | Steep 1/4 cup dried hibiscus in hot simple syrup 15 min, strain | Rum, gin, tequila, sparkling, lemonade |
| **Brown sugar syrup** | 1:1 brown sugar : water | Dark rum, bourbon, coffee, fall cocktails |
| **Vanilla syrup** | Split vanilla bean in simple syrup, steep 30 min | Vodka, bourbon, coffee, cream cocktails |
| **Mint syrup** | Blend fresh mint into cooled simple syrup, fine strain | Rum, bourbon, gin, lemonade |
| **Peach syrup** | Simmer diced ripe peaches in simple syrup 15 min, strain | Bourbon, rum, sparkling wine, sweet tea |

#### Infusion Basics

Infusing spirits with flavor is simple and dramatically expands your range.

**Method**: add ingredients to spirit in a sealed jar. Taste daily. Strain
when the flavor is where you want it.

| Infusion | Spirit | Time | Notes |
|----------|--------|------|-------|
| **Chili (Fresno, jalapeño)** | Tequila, mezcal, vodka | 1–3 days | Check daily — heat builds fast. Remove seeds for less heat |
| **Vanilla bean** | Bourbon, rum, vodka | 1–2 weeks | Split bean lengthwise. One bean per 8 oz |
| **Citrus peel** | Gin, vodka, tequila | 3–5 days | Peel only (no pith — it's bitter). Lemon, lime, grapefruit, orange |
| **Fresh herbs** | Gin, vodka | 2–4 hours | Herbs go muddy fast — taste every hour. Basil, rosemary, thyme |
| **Pineapple** | Rum, tequila, mezcal | 3–5 days | Chunks, including core. Incredible depth |
| **Coffee beans** | Bourbon, rum, vodka | 24–48 hours | Whole beans, lightly crushed. Don't over-extract (bitter) |
| **Stone fruit** | Bourbon, brandy | 3–7 days | Sliced peaches, plums, or apricots. Add a cinnamon stick |
| **Cucumber** | Gin, vodka | 4–8 hours | Sliced, seeded. Goes bitter if left too long |
| **Black tea** | Bourbon, rum | 2–4 hours | Earl Grey bourbon is phenomenal. Short steep — tannins build fast |

## Non-Alcoholic Pairings

Non-alcoholic pairing is an art in its own right — not an afterthought. The
same principles apply: complement, contrast, or bridge.

### Agua Frescas

Traditional Mexican fruit waters — light, refreshing, endlessly adaptable.

**Base method**: blend fruit + water + sugar + lime, strain if desired.

| Agua Fresca | Flavor | Pairs With |
|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Horchata** | Rice milk, cinnamon, vanilla | Spicy food (cools heat), BBQ, Mexican, soul food |
| **Jamaica (hibiscus)** | Tart, cranberry-like, floral | Grilled meats, tacos, rich dishes, fried foods |
| **Tamarindo** | Sweet-tart, complex, tangy | Spicy food, seafood, grilled chicken |
| **Limón (limeade)** | Bright, clean, citrusy | Everything — the universal pairing |
| **Sandía (watermelon)** | Sweet, light, refreshing | Light salads, grilled fish, summer food |
| **Pepino (cucumber-lime)** | Cool, clean, herbal | Spicy food, seafood, Asian-inspired dishes |
| **Mango** | Tropical, sweet, rich | Caribbean, Indian, Thai food, jerk chicken |

### Tea Pairing

Tea follows wine-like logic — body matches body, tannins cut richness.

| Tea | Character | Pairs With |
|-----|-----------|-----------|
| **Green tea** | Light, vegetal, grassy | Japanese food, steamed dishes, light fish, salads |
| **Jasmine tea** | Floral, delicate, sweet | Chinese food, dim sum, light desserts |
| **Oolong** | Complex, partially oxidized, fruity-nutty | Chinese and Taiwanese food, roasted meats, cheese |
| **Black tea (English Breakfast)** | Bold, malty, tannic | British food, fried foods, rich breakfasts, baked goods |
| **Chai (masala)** | Spiced, warming, milky | Indian food, baked goods, fall/winter dishes, brunch |
| **Earl Grey** | Bergamot, floral, citrusy | Scones, light pastry, afternoon tea sandwiches |
| **Rooibos** | Honey, vanilla, caffeine-free | Desserts, evening meals, cheese, Southern food |
| **Pu-erh** | Earthy, deep, fermented | Rich braised meats, aged cheese, mushroom dishes |
| **Mint tea** | Cool, refreshing, digestive | North African food, after heavy meals, lamb, Middle Eastern |
| **Ginger tea** | Spicy, warming | Asian food, sushi, heavy meals (digestive), cold-weather food |

### Lemonade & Shrubs

**Classic lemonade**: 1 cup lemon juice : 1 cup sugar : 4 cups water.
Infinitely adaptable — add herbs, fruit, spice.

**Shrubs (drinking vinegars)**: fruit + sugar + vinegar, aged 1–3 days.
Mixed with sparkling water for a complex, tangy, probiotic-friendly drink.

| Shrub | Vinegar | Fruit | Pairs With |
|-------|---------|-------|-----------|
| **Strawberry-balsamic** | Balsamic | Strawberry | Rich meats, cheese plates, brunch |
| **Peach-apple cider** | Apple cider vinegar | Peach | BBQ, fried chicken, Southern food, summer grilling |
| **Raspberry-red wine** | Red wine vinegar | Raspberry | Chocolate desserts, duck, lamb |
| **Pineapple-lime** | White wine vinegar | Pineapple | Tacos, Caribbean food, jerk chicken |
| **Ginger-lemon** | Apple cider vinegar | Ginger, lemon | Asian food, fried foods, as a palate cleanser |

### Coffee Pairing

Coffee is primarily a dessert and breakfast companion, but it pairs
surprisingly well with savory food when matched correctly.

| Coffee Style | Character | Pairs With |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Light roast (pour over)** | Fruity, floral, bright | Light pastries, fruit tarts, brunch, goat cheese |
| **Medium roast (drip)** | Balanced, nutty, caramel | Classic breakfast, muffins, eggs, pancakes |
| **Dark roast (French press)** | Bold, smoky, chocolate | Chocolate desserts, biscotti, rich breakfast, bbq (unexpected but excellent) |
| **Espresso** | Intense, concentrated | Tiramisu, dark chocolate, after any heavy meal |
| **Cold brew** | Smooth, sweet, low acid | Brunch, donuts, ice cream, weekend mornings |

## Creating Custom Non-Alcoholic Drinks

### The Same 5-Component Framework

Non-alcoholic drinks follow the same structure as cocktails — just replace
the spirit with a flavorful base.

| Component | Alcoholic | Non-Alcoholic Equivalent |
|-----------|-----------|------------------------|
| **Base** | Spirit | Strong tea, coffee, juice, shrub, coconut water |
| **Sweet** | Syrup, liqueur | Same syrups — honey, ginger, lavender, fruit |
| **Sour** | Citrus juice | Same — lemon, lime, grapefruit, tamarind |
| **Bitter** | Bitters, amaro | Non-alcoholic bitters, tonic syrup, grapefruit, coffee |
| **Lengthener** | Soda, tonic | Same — sparkling water, tonic, ginger beer |

### Non-Alcoholic Templates

| Template | Formula | Examples |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Mock Sour** | 4 oz strong tea or juice : 1 oz sour : 0.75 oz sweet syrup | Virgin Whiskey Sour (black tea + lemon + honey) |
| **Spritz** | 2 oz shrub or flavored syrup : top with sparkling water : citrus | Hibiscus Spritz, Peach Ginger Spritz |
| **Smash** | Muddled fruit/herbs + sweetener + soda | Virgin Mojito, Cucumber Mint Smash |
| **Arnold Palmer** | 1:1 lemonade : iced tea | Classic, or riff with flavored syrups |
| **Italian Soda** | 2 oz flavored syrup : sparkling water : cream (optional) | Lavender soda, vanilla cream soda |
| **Lassi** | Yogurt + water/milk + sweet or savory seasoning | Mango lassi, salted cumin lassi |

```
**Inspiration**: [What cuisine, flavor, or dish inspired this drink]
**Serves**: [number]
**Prep Time**: [minutes]

#### Ingredients
- [amount] [ingredient]
- [amount] [ingredient]

#### Method
1. [Step with sensory cues]
2. [Step]

#### Why This Works
[2-3 sentences on the flavor science: what compounds interact,
why the balance works, what the cultural connection is]

#### Variations
- [Alcoholic ↔ non-alcoholic swap]
- [Seasonal variation]
- [Flavor twist]
```
