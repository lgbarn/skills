## West African

### Signature Flavor Notes
Bold, earthy, deeply savory with layers of heat. Peanuts and palm oil provide
richness. Fermented locust beans (dawadawa) add umami depth.

### Essential Pantry
- **Fats**: red palm oil, peanut oil, shea butter
- **Aromatics**: onion, garlic, ginger, scotch bonnet peppers, tomato
- **Starches**: yam, cassava, plantain, rice, millet
- **Proteins**: goat, chicken, dried fish, cowpeas/black-eyed peas, peanuts
- **Fermented**: dawadawa (locust beans), ogiri (fermented sesame)

### Key Techniques
- Slow frying aromatics in palm oil to build flavor base
- Pounding with mortar and pestle (essential texture tool)
- One-pot cooking over wood fire
- Smoking and drying fish/meat for preservation
- Fermenting seeds and beans for condiments

### Classic Dishes
- **Jollof rice** (Nigeria/Ghana): tomato-based one-pot rice, the king of West African dishes
- **Groundnut soup** (Ghana/Nigeria): peanut-based stew with chicken or goat
- **Egusi soup** (Nigeria): melon seed-thickened soup with greens and protein
- **Thieboudienne** (Senegal): stuffed fish over broken rice with vegetables
- **Fufu and light soup** (Ghana): pounded starch with pepper-tomato soup
- **Suya** (Nigeria): spiced grilled meat skewers with yaji spice blend
- **Waakye** (Ghana): rice and beans cooked with millet leaf stalk

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs naturally with Southern/soul food (ancestral connection), Caribbean, Brazilian.

## Ethiopian / East African

### Signature Flavor Notes
Warm, complex, deeply aromatic. Layered spice blends with butter and slow-cooked
alliums create extraordinary depth without overwhelming heat.

### Essential Pantry
- **Spice blends**: berbere (warm and complex), mitmita (fiery)
- **Fats**: niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter with turmeric, cardamom, fenugreek)
- **Grains**: teff (for injera), barley, wheat
- **Legumes**: lentils (red, brown, green), split peas, chickpeas
- **Aromatics**: onion (massive quantities, slow-cooked), garlic, ginger

### Key Techniques
- Slow-cooking onions until deeply caramelized (the foundation of most wots)
- Making berbere from whole spices (toasting, grinding, blending)
- Fermenting teff batter for injera (3-day ferment)
- Building niter kibbeh (infusing butter with spices)
- Communal eating from shared injera platter

### Classic Dishes
- **Doro wot**: chicken stew with berbere and hard-boiled eggs
- **Misir wot**: spiced red lentil stew
- **Kitfo**: Ethiopian steak tartare with mitmita and niter kibbeh
- **Tibs**: sauteed meat with vegetables and rosemary
- **Injera**: spongy fermented teff flatbread (plate and utensil in one)
- **Shiro**: chickpea flour stew (vegan staple)
- **Coffee ceremony**: roasting, grinding, and brewing in jebena

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with Indian (spice complexity), Middle Eastern, North African, Yemeni.

## North African

### Signature Flavor Notes
Warm spice, preserved citrus, floral aromatics, sweet-savory interplay. Rose water
and orange blossom add fragrance. Harissa brings controlled heat.

### Essential Pantry
- **Spice blends**: ras el hanout (the shopkeeper's blend — 20+ spices), harissa paste
- **Preserved**: preserved lemons, dried figs, apricots, dates, olives
- **Grains**: couscous (hand-rolled semolina), flatbreads
- **Proteins**: lamb, chicken, merguez sausage, chickpeas
- **Aromatics**: cumin, coriander, cinnamon, saffron, rose water, orange blossom water

### Key Techniques
- Tagine slow-cooking (conical lid traps and recirculates steam)
- Hand-rolling couscous (steaming multiple times)
- Building chermoula marinades (herb + spice paste)
- Preserving lemons in salt (30+ day cure)
- Layering sweet and savory (fruit + meat in tagines)

### Classic Dishes
- **Tagine** (Morocco): slow-cooked stew — chicken with preserved lemon and olives
- **Couscous royale**: steamed couscous with lamb, chicken, merguez, vegetables
- **Shakshuka** (Tunisia/Libya): eggs poached in spiced tomato sauce
- **Harira** (Morocco): tomato-lentil soup, traditional Ramadan iftar dish
- **Pastilla/B'stilla** (Morocco): savory-sweet pigeon or chicken pie with almonds
- **Merguez**: spiced lamb sausage with harissa

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with Middle Eastern, French (colonial history), Sub-Saharan African, Spanish.

## Caribbean

### Signature Flavor Notes
Tropical, aromatic heat, African and Indian influences merged with indigenous
and European. Allspice and scotch bonnet define the palette.

### Essential Pantry
- **Heat**: scotch bonnet peppers, habaneros
- **Aromatics**: allspice (pimento), thyme, scallions, garlic, ginger
- **Tropical**: coconut milk, plantains, mango, lime, tamarind
- **Proteins**: goat, chicken, salt fish (cod), oxtail, jerk pork
- **Starches**: rice, yam, breadfruit, cassava, dumplings

### Key Techniques
- Jerk: marinating in allspice-scotch bonnet paste, smoking over pimento wood
- Curry (Trinidadian/Jamaican): Indian-influenced, heavier on allspice and scotch bonnet
- One-pot rice and peas (coconut milk + kidney beans or pigeon peas)
- Slow-braising oxtail and goat
- Festival and dumpling frying

### Classic Dishes
- **Jerk chicken/pork** (Jamaica): allspice + scotch bonnet + thyme + pimento wood smoke
- **Curry goat** (Jamaica/Trinidad): Indian-Caribbean fusion curry
- **Rice and peas** (Jamaica): coconut rice with kidney beans, thyme, allspice
- **Ackee and saltfish** (Jamaica): national dish, scrambled ackee fruit with salt cod
- **Doubles** (Trinidad): curried chickpeas in fried flatbread
- **Oxtail stew**: braised with butter beans, allspice, thyme
- **Roti** (Trinidad): curry-filled flatbread wraps

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with West African (ancestral), Indian, soul food, Latin American.

## Mexican

### Signature Flavor Notes
Earthy, bright, layered heat from diverse dried chilies. Corn is sacred.
Complexity built through chili taxonomy rather than spice blends.

### Essential Pantry
- **Dried chilies**: ancho (sweet, mild), guajillo (bright, moderate), pasilla (dark, rich),
  chipotle (smoky), morita (fruity-smoky), arbol (sharp heat), habanero (Yucatan)
- **Fresh**: lime, cilantro, tomatillo, tomato, white onion, serrano, jalapeno
- **Staples**: corn masa (nixtamalized), dried beans (black, pinto), rice
- **Seasonings**: cumin, Mexican oregano (different from Mediterranean), epazote, achiote

### Key Techniques
- Toasting and rehydrating dried chilies (foundational technique)
- Nixtamalization: treating corn with calcium hydroxide (makes masa, frees niacin)
- Building mole (20+ ingredients, multi-day process for complex versions)
- Charring vegetables for salsa (comal, open flame)
- Slow-braising in chile sauce (barbacoa, birria)

### Classic Dishes
- **Mole** (Oaxaca/Puebla): complex sauce — mole negro, mole poblano, mole rojo
- **Tacos al pastor**: Lebanon-influenced spit-roasted pork with pineapple
- **Birria** (Jalisco): chile-braised beef or goat, served with consomme
- **Tamales**: masa dough filled with meat/cheese, steamed in corn husk
- **Pozole**: hominy soup with pork, garnished with cabbage, radish, lime
- **Chiles en nogada** (Puebla): stuffed poblano with walnut cream, pomegranate
- **Elote**: grilled corn with mayo, cotija, chili, lime

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with Middle Eastern (tacos al pastor/shawarma connection), Caribbean, Texan, soul food.

## French

### Signature Flavor Notes
Classical technique-driven, built on stocks, sauces, and butter. Regional diversity
from rich cream-based Northern dishes to olive oil-bright Provencal cooking.

### Essential Pantry
- **Fats**: butter (unsalted), olive oil (Southern), duck fat, cream
- **Aromatics**: mirepoix (onion, carrot, celery), shallots, garlic, leeks
- **Herbs**: thyme, bay leaf, tarragon, chervil, parsley, herbs de Provence
- **Wine**: dry white, red Burgundy, Champagne (for sauces)
- **Stocks**: chicken, veal, fish, vegetable (fond = foundation)

### Key Techniques
- **The 5 mother sauces**: bechamel, veloute, espagnole, hollandaise, tomato
- Building fond (caramelized bits) and deglazing
- Emulsion sauces (hollandaise, vinaigrette, beurre blanc)
- Braising, confit, en papillote
- Classical knife cuts (brunoise, julienne, chiffonade, mirepoix)

### Classic Dishes
- **Coq au vin**: chicken braised in red wine with lardons, mushrooms, pearl onions
- **Boeuf bourguignon**: beef braised in Burgundy wine
- **Ratatouille** (Provence): layered summer vegetables in olive oil and herbs
- **Cassoulet** (Southwest): white bean and meat casserole
- **Soupe a l'oignon**: onion soup with Gruyere crouton gratin
- **Bouillabaisse** (Marseille): saffron fish stew

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with Italian, North African (colonial), Vietnamese (colonial), Belgian.

## Italian

### Signature Flavor Notes
Bold aromatics, herbaceous, ingredient-quality-driven. Simplicity elevated —
few ingredients, each at peak quality. Strong North-South regional divide.

### Essential Pantry
- **Fats**: extra virgin olive oil, butter (Northern)
- **Aromatics**: soffritto (onion, carrot, celery), garlic (Southern), shallots
- **Tomatoes**: San Marzano (canned), fresh (summer), tomato paste (concentrate)
- **Cheese**: Parmigiano-Reggiano, Pecorino Romano, mozzarella, ricotta
- **Herbs**: basil (fresh), oregano (dried), rosemary, sage

### Key Techniques
- Soffritto as flavor base (low heat, patience)
- Pasta water management (starchy water finishes sauces)
- Emulsifying pasta sauces (fat + starchy water + tossing)
- Wood-fired high-heat cooking (pizza, bread)
- Preserving (curing meats, sun-dried tomatoes, oil-packed vegetables)

### Classic Dishes
- **Cacio e pepe** (Rome): pecorino + black pepper + pasta water emulsion
- **Ragu Bolognese** (Emilia-Romagna): slow-cooked meat sauce with milk and wine
- **Risotto alla Milanese** (Lombardy): saffron risotto with bone marrow
- **Pizza Margherita** (Naples): tomato, mozzarella, basil
- **Osso buco** (Milan): braised veal shanks with gremolata
- **Pesto alla Genovese** (Liguria): basil, pine nuts, Parmigiano, garlic, olive oil

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with French, Greek, Spanish, North African.

## Indian

### Signature Flavor Notes
Complex layered spice with enormous regional diversity. North: cream, yogurt, tandoor.
South: coconut, curry leaves, tamarind, rice-forward. Spice LAYERING is the key
technique — same spice at different stages produces different effects.

### Essential Pantry
- **Whole spices**: cumin seeds, mustard seeds, coriander seeds, cardamom, cloves,
  cinnamon sticks, fenugreek seeds, black peppercorns
- **Ground blends**: garam masala, turmeric, chili powder, chaat masala
- **Wet**: ginger-garlic paste, yogurt, coconut milk, tamarind paste
- **Fresh**: curry leaves, cilantro, green chilies, mint
- **Lentils/legumes**: toor dal, chana dal, masoor dal, chickpeas, kidney beans

### Key Techniques
- **Tempering (tadka/chaunk)**: blooming whole spices in hot oil or ghee
- Layered spice addition: whole spices first, ground spices with onions, garam masala at end
- Slow-cooking onions to different stages (translucent, golden, deep brown)
- Tandoor oven cooking (extremely high dry heat, 480°F+)
- Marinating in yogurt + spice pastes

### Classic Dishes
- **Butter chicken** (Punjab): tandoor chicken in creamy tomato sauce
- **Biryani** (Hyderabad): layered rice and meat, dum-cooked (sealed pot)
- **Dal makhani** (Punjab): black lentils slow-cooked 24+ hours with cream
- **Dosa** (South India): fermented rice-lentil crepe with sambar and chutney
- **Chana masala** (North): chickpeas in spiced tomato-onion sauce
- **Rogan josh** (Kashmir): slow-braised lamb in Kashmiri chili sauce

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with Ethiopian (spice complexity), Middle Eastern, Thai, Caribbean (Trinidadian).

## Thai

### Signature Flavor Notes
The four pillars: sweet, sour, salty, spicy — every dish seeks dynamic balance.
Aromatic herbs provide a fragrant top note. Food should excite all senses at once.

### Essential Pantry
- **Aromatics**: lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, Thai basil, cilantro root
- **Sauces**: fish sauce (nam pla), oyster sauce, soy sauce (thin and dark)
- **Sweet**: palm sugar, coconut sugar
- **Heat**: bird's eye chili, dried chili flakes, chili paste (nam prik)
- **Acid**: lime juice, tamarind paste, rice vinegar
- **Coconut**: coconut milk, coconut cream

### Key Techniques
- Mortar-and-pestle curry paste (pounding, not blending — texture matters)
- High-heat wok stir-frying (pad)
- Balancing the four taste pillars by tasting and adjusting continuously
- Tempering coconut cream (cracking the cream before adding curry paste)
- Carving and garnishing (presentation is integral)

### Classic Dishes
- **Tom yum goong**: hot-and-sour shrimp soup
- **Green curry (gaeng khiao wan)**: coconut curry with green chili paste
- **Pad Thai**: stir-fried rice noodles with tamarind sauce
- **Som tum**: green papaya salad (all four pillars in one dish)
- **Massaman curry**: Persian-influenced curry with peanuts and potato
- **Larb**: minced meat salad with herbs, lime, roasted rice powder
- **Khao soi**: Northern Thai coconut curry noodle soup

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with Vietnamese, Lao, Malaysian, Indian (Southern), Burmese.

## Chinese

### Signature Flavor Notes
Wok hei (breath of the wok), balance, and enormous regional diversity.
Eight major regional cuisines, each with distinct flavor profiles.

### Essential Pantry
- **Sauces**: light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, black vinegar
- **Aromatics**: ginger, scallion, garlic (the "holy trinity" of Chinese cooking)
- **Spices**: five-spice powder (star anise, clove, cinnamon, Sichuan peppercorn, fennel)
- **Fermented**: doubanjiang (chili bean paste), fermented black beans, tofu
- **Staples**: jasmine rice, wheat noodles, tofu, sesame oil

### Key Techniques
- High-heat wok stir-frying (achieving wok hei)
- Velveting protein (cornstarch + egg white + oil marinade for silky texture)
- Red-braising (soy sauce, sugar, star anise — Shanghai/Hunan)
- Steaming (dim sum, fish, buns)
- Tea-smoking, clay pot cooking

### Regional Highlights
- **Sichuan**: mala (numbing-spicy), doubanjiang, Sichuan peppercorn, chili oil
- **Cantonese**: clean, fresh, wok hei-focused, steamed, dim sum, seafood
- **Hunan**: xiang la (fragrant-spicy), smoked, cured, pickled
- **Shandong**: vinegar, scallion, braised, ocean influence

### Classic Dishes
- **Mapo tofu** (Sichuan): silken tofu in mala chili-bean sauce with ground pork
- **Kung Pao chicken** (Sichuan): diced chicken with peanuts, chili, Sichuan peppercorn
- **Char siu** (Cantonese): honey-glazed barbecue pork
- **Xiaolongbao** (Shanghai): soup dumplings
- **Peking duck** (Beijing): lacquered duck with thin pancakes

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with Japanese, Korean, Vietnamese, Thai, Malaysian.

## Japanese

### Signature Flavor Notes
Clean umami, minimalist, seasonality-driven (shun). Enhance natural flavors,
never mask them. Precision and restraint define the approach.

### Essential Pantry
- **Dashi**: kombu (kelp) + katsuobushi (bonito flakes) — the umami foundation
- **Soy**: soy sauce (shoyu), miso (white, red, mixed), tamari
- **Rice**: short-grain rice, rice vinegar, mirin (sweet rice wine), sake
- **Sea**: nori, wakame, bonito flakes, various fish
- **Vegetables**: daikon, shiso, lotus root, burdock root, shiitake

### Key Techniques
- Dashi extraction (ichiban dashi vs. niban dashi — first vs. second extraction)
- Precision knife work (katsuramuki, sengiri, rangiri)
- Rice washing and cooking (precise water ratios, resting)
- Gentle simmering with drop lid (otoshibuta)
- Fermentation (miso, soy sauce, natto, pickles)

### Classic Dishes
- **Miso soup**: dashi base with miso, tofu, wakame, scallion
- **Ramen**: regional styles — tonkotsu (Hakata), shoyu (Tokyo), shio, miso (Sapporo)
- **Sushi/sashimi**: vinegared rice and pristine raw fish
- **Tempura**: light, crispy battered and fried vegetables/seafood
- **Tonkatsu**: breaded deep-fried pork cutlet with tonkatsu sauce
- **Gyudon**: simmered beef and onion over rice

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with Korean, Chinese, Peruvian (Nikkei), French (shared precision).

## Korean

### Signature Flavor Notes
Fermented depth, balanced spicy-sweet, sesame nuttiness, banchan harmony.
Every meal is about the ensemble of small dishes, not a single hero.

### Essential Pantry
- **Chili**: gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes), gochujang (fermented chili paste)
- **Fermented**: doenjang (soybean paste), ganjang (soy sauce), jeotgal (fermented seafood)
- **Sesame**: sesame oil, sesame seeds (toasted, omnipresent)
- **Aromatics**: garlic (massive quantities), ginger, scallion
- **Vegetables**: napa cabbage, Korean radish, perilla leaves, bean sprouts

### Key Techniques
- Kimchi fermentation (napa cabbage, radish, seasonal variations)
- Korean BBQ (tabletop grilling with lettuce wraps)
- Jjigae (stew) and tang (soup) building
- Banchan preparation (small side dishes, 3–12 per meal)
- Quick-pickling and namul (seasoned vegetable sides)

### Classic Dishes
- **Kimchi jjigae**: fermented kimchi stew with pork and tofu
- **Bulgogi**: marinated thin-sliced beef, grilled
- **Bibimbap**: mixed rice with vegetables, gochujang, fried egg, meat
- **Japchae**: sweet potato glass noodles with vegetables and sesame
- **Tteokbokki**: spicy stir-fried rice cakes in gochujang sauce
- **Samgyeopsal**: grilled pork belly with lettuce wraps and ssamjang

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with Japanese, Chinese, Mexican (shared love of fermented + spicy).

## Middle Eastern

### Signature Flavor Notes
Warm, tangy, aromatic with generous use of fresh herbs, citrus, and
warm spices. Mezze culture emphasizes sharing and variety.

### Essential Pantry
- **Spice blends**: za'atar (thyme, sumac, sesame), baharat (warm blend), ras el hanout
- **Sour**: sumac, pomegranate molasses, lemon juice
- **Tahini**: sesame paste — used in sweet and savory applications
- **Fresh herbs**: parsley (massive quantities), mint, dill, cilantro
- **Proteins**: lamb, chicken, chickpeas, lentils, yogurt

### Key Techniques
- Grilling over charcoal (kebab, shawarma, kofta)
- Building tahini sauces and dressings
- Flatbread baking (pita, lavash, manakish)
- Mezze composition (balancing flavors across small plates)
- Stuffing vegetables (mahshi — grape leaves, zucchini, peppers)

### Classic Dishes
- **Hummus**: chickpea-tahini puree with olive oil and paprika
- **Shawarma**: spit-roasted meat (chicken or lamb), sliced thin
- **Falafel**: deep-fried chickpea or fava bean fritters
- **Fattoush**: bread salad with sumac dressing
- **Kibbeh**: bulgur and lamb croquettes
- **Musakhan** (Palestine): sumac chicken on taboon bread with onions
- **Baklava**: phyllo pastry with nuts and syrup

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with North African, Greek, Turkish, Indian, Ethiopian.

## Brazilian

### Signature Flavor Notes
Tropical, smoky, African-rooted (especially Bahian cuisine). Massive regional
diversity from Amazonian to Gaucho to Northeastern.

### Essential Pantry
- **Fats**: dende oil (red palm oil, Bahian), olive oil, butter
- **Staples**: black beans, rice, farofa (toasted cassava flour), cassava
- **Proteins**: beef (churrasco cuts), chicken, salt cod (bacalhau), shrimp
- **Tropical**: coconut milk, lime, passion fruit, acai, hearts of palm
- **Aromatics**: garlic, onion, bay leaves, cilantro, malagueta pepper

### Key Techniques
- Churrasco (rotisserie-grilled meats over charcoal, salt-crusted)
- Bahian stewing in dende oil and coconut milk
- Feijoada construction (layered black bean stew, multiple meats)
- Farofa toasting (buttered cassava flour with additions)
- Tropical fruit preparation and juice/cocktail mixing

### Classic Dishes
- **Feijoada**: black bean stew with pork, sausage, dried beef (national dish)
- **Moqueca** (Bahia): fish or shrimp stew with dende oil and coconut milk
- **Churrasco**: mixed grilled meats (picanha, fraldinha, costela)
- **Pao de queijo**: cheese bread made with tapioca flour
- **Acaraje** (Bahia): fried black-eyed pea fritters (West African origin — akara)
- **Brigadeiro**: chocolate truffle balls (national dessert)

### Cuisine Affinities
Pairs with West African (Bahian connection), Portuguese, soul food, Japanese (Sao Paulo Nikkei).
