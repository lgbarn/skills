# Economic Calendar Reference

Comprehensive guide to high-impact US economic events that affect futures markets.

---

## Overview

Economic events can cause sudden volatility spikes that violate trading assumptions. For prop firm trading (especially Apex Trader Funding with strict drawdown rules), avoiding these periods is critical for capital preservation.

**Recommendation:** Enable news filter (`NEWS_FILTER_ENABLED=true`) to automatically skip trading during high-impact events.

---

## Event Impact Levels

### High Impact ⚠️
- Major volatility expected (50+ point moves in NQ)
- Blackout recommended: 30-90 minutes before/after
- Can breach stop losses with slippage
- **Always avoid** for prop firm accounts

### Medium Impact ⚡
- Moderate volatility (20-50 point moves)
- Consider wider stops or skip
- Watch price action closely

### Low Impact
- Minimal effect on intraday trading
- Safe to trade through
- Monitor for surprises

## Monthly High-Impact Events

### Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) 🔥

**Schedule:** First Friday of each month, 8:30 AM ET

**What it is:** Monthly employment report showing jobs added/lost

**Why it matters:** Most important monthly economic indicator. Shows labor market strength.

**Typical Reaction:**
- Immediate 50-150 point spike in NQ
- Direction depends on vs. estimate
- Volatility continues 1-2 hours after

**Trading Recommendation:**
- **Blackout:** 8:00 AM - 10:00 AM ET
- If trading: Wait 30+ minutes after release
- Expect wide spreads and slippage

**Example Dates 2026:**
- January 2 (Friday)
- February 6 (Friday)
- March 6 (Friday)
- April 3 (Friday)
- May 1 (Friday)
- June 5 (Friday)
- July 2 (Friday) - Holiday week
- August 7 (Friday)
- September 4 (Friday)
- October 2 (Friday)
- November 6 (Friday)
- December 4 (Friday)

### Consumer Price Index (CPI) 🔥

**Schedule:** Mid-month (~13th), 8:30 AM ET

**What it is:** Monthly inflation report

**Why it matters:** Fed uses CPI to guide interest rate decisions. Directly affects Fed policy expectations.

**Typical Reaction:**
- 100+ point moves in NQ common
- "Hotter than expected" = sell-off (higher rates)
- "Cooler than expected" = rally (lower rates)

**Trading Recommendation:**
- **Blackout:** 8:00 AM - 9:30 AM ET (minimum)
- Consider avoiding entire morning session
- Watch for multi-day trend changes

**Components to Watch:**
- **Core CPI** (excludes food/energy) - Fed's preferred measure
- **MoM (Month-over-Month)** - Short-term trend
- **YoY (Year-over-Year)** - Longer-term trend

---

### FOMC Rate Decision 🔥🔥🔥

**Schedule:** 8 times per year, 2:00 PM ET

**What it is:** Federal Reserve announces interest rate decision

**Why it matters:** THE most important economic event. Directly sets monetary policy.

**Typical Reaction:**
- Immediate 200+ point spike (either direction)
- Press conference at 2:30 PM adds volatility
- Can see 500+ point range on surprise changes

**Trading Recommendation:**
- **Blackout:** 1:30 PM - 4:00 PM ET (entire afternoon)
- Do NOT trade FOMC days if possible
- If you must trade, exit all positions by 1:00 PM

**2026 FOMC Schedule:**
- January 28-29 (Wed-Thu) - Decision at 2:00 PM Thu
- March 17-18 (Tue-Wed) - Decision at 2:00 PM Wed
- April 28-29 (Tue-Wed) - Decision at 2:00 PM Wed
- June 16-17 (Tue-Wed) - Decision at 2:00 PM Wed
- July 28-29 (Tue-Wed) - Decision at 2:00 PM Wed
- September 15-16 (Tue-Wed) - Decision at 2:00 PM Wed
- October 27-28 (Tue-Wed) - Decision at 2:00 PM Wed
- December 9-10 (Wed-Thu) - Decision at 2:00 PM Thu

**What to Watch:**
- **Rate change** - Expected vs. actual
- **Dot plot** (quarterly) - Future rate projections
- **Fed statement** - Policy guidance wording changes
- **Press conference** - Fed Chair Q&A (live interpretation)

### Producer Price Index (PPI)

**Schedule:** Mid-month (~14th), 8:30 AM ET

**What it is:** Wholesale inflation (producer level)

**Why it matters:** Leading indicator for CPI (producer costs → consumer costs)

**Typical Reaction:**
- 30-80 point moves in NQ
- Less impact than CPI but still significant
- Can preview CPI trend

**Trading Recommendation:**
- **Blackout:** 8:00 AM - 9:00 AM ET
- Lower priority than CPI but still avoid

---

### Retail Sales

**Schedule:** Mid-month (~15th), 8:30 AM ET

**What it is:** Consumer spending report

**Why it matters:** Consumer spending = 70% of US GDP

**Typical Reaction:**
- 40-100 point moves
- "Strong sales" = bullish (economy growing)
- "Weak sales" = bearish (recession risk)

**Trading Recommendation:**
- **Blackout:** 8:00 AM - 9:00 AM ET
- Safe to resume after initial reaction

---

### ISM Manufacturing PMI

**Schedule:** First business day of month, 10:00 AM ET

**What it is:** Manufacturing sector health survey

**Why it matters:** Forward-looking economic indicator

**Typical Reaction:**
- 30-60 point moves
- PMI > 50 = expansion (bullish)
- PMI < 50 = contraction (bearish)

**Trading Recommendation:**
- **Blackout:** 9:30 AM - 10:30 AM ET
- Lower impact than other reports

---

### ISM Services PMI

**Schedule:** Third business day of month, 10:00 AM ET

**What it is:** Services sector health survey (larger than manufacturing)

**Why it matters:** Services = 80% of US economy

**Typical Reaction:**
- 30-60 point moves
- Similar to Manufacturing PMI

**Trading Recommendation:**
- **Blackout:** 9:30 AM - 10:30 AM ET

### Fed Chair Testimony

**Schedule:** Quarterly, usually 10:00 AM ET

**What it is:** Fed Chair testifies before Congress

**Why it matters:** Policy guidance and economic outlook

**Typical Reaction:**
- 50-150 point moves during testimony
- Live interpretation of comments
- Q&A can cause volatility

**Trading Recommendation:**
- **Blackout:** Entire session when scheduled
- Check Fed calendar: https://www.federalreserve.gov/

## Weekly Events

### Initial Jobless Claims

**Schedule:** Every Thursday, 8:30 AM ET

**What it is:** Weekly unemployment insurance claims

**Why it matters:** Real-time labor market data

**Typical Reaction:**
- 10-30 point moves (usually low impact)
- Can spike on unexpected readings
- More important during recessions

**Trading Recommendation:**
- Usually safe to trade through
- If claims spike unexpectedly, treat as high-impact

## Blackout Strategy by Account Mode

### Eval Mode (Aggressive)

You need to hit profit target quickly, so you might take more event risk:

- **Must Avoid:** FOMC, NFP, CPI
- **Consider Trading:** PPI, Retail Sales, ISM (with tight stops)
- **Blackout Window:** Minimum (15-20 minutes)

**Risk:** Higher chance of drawdown breach

**Reward:** More trading opportunities to hit target

### PA Mode (Conservative)

Funded account requires capital preservation:

- **Must Avoid:** FOMC, NFP, CPI, PPI, GDP
- **Consider Trading:** Only non-high-impact events
- **Blackout Window:** Extended (45-60 minutes)

**Risk:** Fewer trading days

**Reward:** Lower chance of account-ending drawdown

## Trading During Events (Advanced)

If you choose to trade during/after events:

### Wait for Initial Reaction

```
8:30 AM - Event released
8:30-8:35 - Violent spike (DO NOT TRADE)
8:35-9:00 - Price discovery phase (EXTREME CAUTION)
9:00-9:30 - Trend establishment (Consider entry if clear)
9:30+ - Normal trading resumes
```

### Use Wider Stops

- Multiply your normal ATR stop by 2-3x
- Expect slippage of 5-15 points
- Reduce position size to maintain same $ risk

### Reduce Position Size

```python
# During high-impact event periods
if news_calendar.is_near_event(current_time, minutes=60):
    contracts = contracts // 2  # Half normal size
```

### Exit Before Events

```python
# If holding position and event approaching
if news_calendar.is_blackout_period(current_time):
    if position != 0:
        logger.warning("Exiting position before news event")
        await exit_position("PreNewsExit")
```

## Bot Integration Example

```python
# In bot_*.py

async def on_bar(self, bar: dict):
    """Process each bar."""

    # Fetch events at start of day
    if bar["timestamp"].hour == 9 and bar["timestamp"].minute == 30:
        await self.news_calendar.fetch_today_events()

        # Log today's events
        events = self.news_calendar.events
        if events:
            logger.warning(f"⚠️ {len(events)} high-impact events today:")
            for event in events:
                logger.warning(f"   {event}")

    # Check blackout
    if self.news_calendar.is_blackout_period(bar["timestamp"]):
        # Exit position if holding
        if self.position != 0 and self.config.news_exit_on_blackout:
            logger.warning("Exiting position: news blackout starting")
            await self._exit_position("NewsBlackout", bar["close"])
        return

    # Normal trading logic
    # ...
```

## Conclusion

Economic events are the #1 cause of unexpected drawdowns in algorithmic trading. For prop firm accounts with strict rules:

1. **Enable news filter** - Automate blackout periods
2. **Conservative is better** - Skip questionable events
3. **Know the schedule** - FOMC dates are published months in advance
4. **Exit before events** - Don't hold positions into high-impact releases
5. **Test your assumptions** - Backtest on historical event days to see impact

**Remember:** Missing a few trading days is better than breaching your drawdown and losing your account.
